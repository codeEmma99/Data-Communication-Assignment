Assignment Summary

Satellite data communication is the use of orbiting satellites to send and receive information over long distances. A ground station sends a signal upward to a satellite, the satellite processes or repeats the signal, and another ground station receives it.

Related YouTube Video

This video explains how communication satellites send, receive, and relay signals between Earth stations.

Key Parts

  • Uplink: signal sent from Earth to the satellite.
  • Transponder: satellite component that receives, amplifies, and retransmits the signal.
  • Downlink: signal sent from the satellite back to Earth.
  • Ground station: antenna system used to send and receive satellite signals.

Why It Matters

Satellite communication supports television broadcasting, GPS, weather forecasting, internet access in remote areas, emergency communication, and global data transfer.
